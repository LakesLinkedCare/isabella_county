Why Clare County

A county where the opioid crisis intersects with aging and poverty

Clare County's burden profile differs from Isabella in a critical way: the population is 7 years older (median 46.8 vs. state 40.0), which amplifies every chronic disease burden and means premature deaths carry fewer remaining life-years lost — but the absolute per-capita DALY rate is still 14% higher than Isabella. The opioid overdose mortality at ~47/100,000 is more than double the national average. COPD at 12.7% is the highest in our comparison set and reflects decades of rural tobacco use in an aging cohort. Clare lacks the university health anchor that Isabella has through CMU — there is no single institutional partner to anchor a telehealth or integrated care program. This makes community health worker and MAT telehealth models the most feasible entry points.
