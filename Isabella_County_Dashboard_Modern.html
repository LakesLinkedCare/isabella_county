Value of Statistical Life (VSL)

~$4.84B
Range: ~$2.3B – $7.5B (HHS 2026 low/high)
Social willingness to pay to reduce premature mortality risk. HHS VSL 2026 ($13.4M) income-adjusted for Isabella County (~$10.5M; elasticity 0.4) × ~461 deaths/year. Method used by EPA, DOT, HHS for regulatory cost-benefit analysis.
Reference LE note: YLL and human capital burden use Michigan's observed life expectancy of 78.6 years (MDHHS 2024: Male 76.2, Female 80.9, average) as a planning-grade approximation, rather than a normative reference life table used in WHO/GBD burden analyses. The WHO Global Health Estimates frontier reference life table is approximately 90 years at birth. Standard YLL uses remaining reference life expectancy at age of death, not life expectancy at birth minus mean age; primary estimate uses WHO GHE frontier LE 89.1 yrs with mental health remission adjustment (×0.50): 15,140 DALYs. c,k YLL simplification: YLL ≈ deaths × (L − mean age at death), where L = 89.1 yrs (WHO GHE frontier, primary standard) or 78.6 yrs (Michigan LE, planning-grade sensitivity). Standard WHO/GBD YLL uses remaining LE at the age of death from a reference life table, summed over age-specific death counts; it does not use LE at birth minus mean age. The WHO Global Health Estimates frontier reference life table is approximately 90 years at birth; this analysis uses WHO GHE frontier LE as primary and Michigan observed LE as sensitivity analysis. Mean ages are national proxies, not county-specific distributions. Treat as planning approximation only.

Key Findings

Sorted by estimated DALY burden and relative gap vs. Michigan average

🚨 Mental Health Access Crisis

Federal Mental Health HPSA designation (Central MI Service Area). HRSA shortage ratio 176,938:1 - 18.9× worse than state average. MH/SUD hospitalizations 3,420/100k. YLL likely underestimated due to suppressed suicide data.

🚨 Stroke Mortality 49% Above State

59.4 vs Michigan 39.9/100k - largest relative gap among tracked conditions. Associated with hypertension, poverty-related access barriers, and limited neurology coverage. Each prevented death corresponds to roughly $10.5M in income-adjusted VSL burden.

🚨 Maternal Health - NAS & Tobacco

NAS rate 583.1/100k live births signals substantial prenatal opioid use disorder. Maternal tobacco use 22.0% is 3.4× national average. Both are strong predictors of intergenerational disease burden.

⚠ Poverty as Root Structural Driver

26.5% below 100% FPL (MDHHS 2020) or 21.9% (Census ACS 2024) - well above state average regardless of source. 45.5% below 200% FPL. Food insecurity 16.4% vs MI 13.7%.

⚠ COPD Mortality 37% Above State

60.6 vs Michigan 44.2/100k. Combined with maternal tobacco 22% and physical inactivity 18.1%, indicates a substantial preventable lung disease burden.

✓ Relative Strengths

Cancer incidence below state and national averages. HIV prevalence lower than Michigan (65.2 vs 163.1/100k). Eviction rate 1.5% vs state 3.3%. Child blood lead (EBLL) 1.4% vs state 3.1%.
