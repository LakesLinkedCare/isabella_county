Human Capital Approach

~$477M
7,696 DALYs × $62,000 (MI GDP per capita 2024)
Estimates foregone productivity due to premature death and disability. Conservative lower bound. Standard in GBD academic literature; enables cross-study comparison. Does not include direct medical costs.

Value of Statistical Life (VSL)

~$4.1B
Range: $1.9B – $6.3B (HHS 2026 low/high)
Societal willingness to pay to prevent premature deaths. HHS VSL 2026 ($13.4M) income-adjusted for Isabella County ($11.6M; elasticity 0.4) × ~357 deaths/year. Method used by EPA, DOT, HHS for regulatory cost-benefit analysis.
Reference LE note: YLL and human capital burden use Michigan's observed life expectancy of 78.6 years (MDHHS 2024: Male 76.2, Female 80.9, average), not the GBD theoretical reference of 88.9 years. Using Michigan's actual LE is methodologically appropriate for local planning — it reflects years residents realistically expect to live. The 8.6× difference between methods is expected: Human Capital = lost productivity ($477M); VSL = societal willingness to pay to prevent deaths ($4.1B). VSL: $13.4M × (55,237/80,000)^0.4 = $11.6M per statistical life. Source: HHS ASPE 2026. LE source: MDHHS, mdch.state.mi.us/osr/deaths/lifeUSMI.asp.

Key Findings

Sorted by estimated DALY burden and relative gap vs. Michigan average

🚨 Mental Health Access Crisis

Federal Mental Health HPSA designation (Central MI Service Area). HRSA shortage ratio 176,938:1 — 18.9× worse than state average. MH/SUD hospitalizations 3,420/100k. YLL likely underestimated due to suppressed suicide data.

🚨 Stroke Mortality 49% Above State

59.4 vs Michigan 39.9/100k — largest relative gap among tracked conditions. Associated with hypertension, poverty-related access barriers, and limited neurology coverage. Each prevented death averts ~$11.6M societal cost (VSL-adjusted).

🚨 Maternal Health — NAS & Tobacco

NAS rate 583.1/100k live births signals substantial prenatal opioid use disorder. Maternal tobacco use 22.0% is 3.4× national average. Both are strong predictors of intergenerational disease burden.

⚠ Poverty as Root Structural Driver

26.5% below 100% FPL (MDHHS 2020) or 21.9% (Census ACS 2024) — well above state average regardless of source. 45.5% below 200% FPL. Food insecurity 16.4% vs MI 13.7%.

⚠ COPD Mortality 37% Above State

60.6 vs Michigan 44.2/100k. Combined with maternal tobacco 22% and physical inactivity 18.1%, indicates a substantial preventable lung disease burden.

✓ Relative Strengths

Cancer incidence below state and national averages. HIV prevalence lower than Michigan (65.2 vs 163.1/100k). Eviction rate 1.5% vs state 3.3%. Child blood lead (EBLL) 1.4% vs state 3.1%.
